📝 Study Tips


  1. 📅 Make a Realistic Timetable.

    • Study for 6–8 hours a day with breaks in between.

    • Divide your time between subjects based on difficulty and exam schedule.

    • Include short breaks (5–10 mins) after every 45–50 minutes.

  2. 📝 Revise Using Active Recall

    • Don't just read — close your book and try to recall what you studied.

    • Use techniques like flashcards, self-quizzing, and summarizing.

    • Create mind maps for big chapters.

  3. ⏳ Use the Pomodoro Technique

    • Study for 25 minutes → Take a 5-minute break.

    • After 4 sessions, take a 15–20 minute break.

    • It improves focus and prevents burnout.

  4. ✅ Practice with Past Papers

    • Solve last 5 years' question papers.

    • Practice writing full answers — don’t just read them.

    • Time yourself to simulate exam pressure.

  5. 📚 Focus on Important Topics

    • Study previous year trends to identify hot topics.

    • Prioritize chapters with more weightage.

    • Ask teachers or friends for most commonly asked questions.

  6. 🧘 Stay Mentally Fresh

    • Avoid late-night cramming.

    • Sleep at least 6–7 hours.

    • Meditate or stretch for 5 minutes during breaks.

  7. 🛑 Avoid These Mistakes

    • Don’t study new topics a day before the exam.

    • Don’t compare your preparation with others.

    • Don’t keep checking your phone while studying.
  • 🧃 Some Additional Tips :
    • Keep a bottle of water and snacks at your study table.

    • Use sticky notes to mark key formulas or rules.

    • Teach someone else — it helps you learn better!
